42===Deep-fried Twinkie===42===Deep-fried Twinkie===
43[[File:Deep-fried twinkie.jpg|thumb|right|A deep-fried Twinkie]]43[[File:Deep-fried twinkie.jpg|thumb|right|A deep-fried Twinkie]]
t44A [[deep frying|deep-fried]] Twinkie involves freezing the cake, dipping it into [[batter (cooking)|batter]], and deep-frying it. A story in ''[[The New York Times]]'' about the deep fried Twinkie with its inventor—Christopher Sell, originally from [[Rugby, Warwickshire|Rugby]], England—described this way: "Something magical occurs when the pastry hits the hot oil. The creamy white vegetable shortening filling liquefies, impregnating the [[sponge cake]] with its luscious vanilla flavor.... The cake itself softens and warms, nearly melting, contrasting with the crisp, deep-fried crust in a buttery and suave way. The ''[[wikt:pièce de résistance|pièce de résistance]]'', however, is a ruby-hued berry sauce, adding a tart sophistication to all that airy sugary goodness."<ref>{{cite news | url =https://www.nytimes.com/2002/05/15/dining/fry-that-twinkie-but-hold-the-chips.html | work=The New York Times | title= Fry That Twinkie, But Hold the Chips |date= May 15, 2002 |access-date=August 30, 2011}}</ref> The [[Texas State Fair]] had introduced the fried Twinkie to great popular acclaim, and the notion spread to other [[state fair]]s across the U.S., as well as some establishments that specialize in fried foods.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://archives.cnn.com/2002/US/West/09/18/offbeat.twinkie.reut/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130124151542/http://archives.cnn.com/2002/US/West/09/18/offbeat.twinkie.reut/|url-status= dead| archive-date={{date|2013-01-24|MDY}} | publisher = CNN | title=New junk food fad: Deep-fried Twinkies |date= September 18, 2002| access-date={{date|2018-07-25|MDY}}}}</ref> Fried Twinkies are sold throughout the U.S. in fairs as well as ball games, and in various restaurants.
