41This album also marked the beginning of the band's legal fight with its record label, Epic. Guitarist, producer and primary songwriter [[Tom Scholz]] claimed that Epic executives pushed him and the band into releasing the album before they felt it was ready. He also said that the album "was ridiculously short. It needed another song". Their next album, ''[[Third Stage]]'', was not released for another eight years, by which time the band and record label had parted ways and were fighting a courtroom battle that Boston ultimately won.41This album also marked the beginning of the band's legal fight with its record label, Epic. 66==Compact disc releases==66==Compact disc releases==
67''Don't Look Back'' was among the first commercially produced [[compact discs]] when the format was introduced in 1983, but because of ongoing legal issues between Scholz and [[CBS Associated Records|CBS Records]], the title was pulled after a small production run and did not reappear on CD until three years later. Inserts for the original CD pressings contained the "spaceship blueprints" from the original album dust jacket; those illustrations were not included in the 1986 reissue.67''Don't Look Back'' was among the first commercially produced [[compact discs]] when the format was introduced in 1983, but because of ongoing legal issues between Scholz and [[CBS Associated Records|CBS Records]], the title was pulled after a small production run and did not reappear on CD until three years later. Inserts for the original CD pressings contained the "spaceship blueprints" from the original album dust jacket; those illustrations were not included in the 1986 reissue.
