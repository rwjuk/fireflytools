n132The SCO Group was, in 2004, involved in a dispute with various Linux vendors and users. In this campaign SCO "announced that Linux contained SCO's UNIX System V source code and that Linux was an unauthorized derivative of UNIX".<ref name="copyrights"/> Although many are skeptical about their claims, SCO initiated a series of lawsuits and claims that so far have not been upheld by the courts. Thus far the impact on both Linux and Unix has been minimal. Further complicating the issue is the legitimacy of SCO claims concerning the ownership of [[SVR4|System V Release 4.0 (SVR4)]] Unix copyrights.
