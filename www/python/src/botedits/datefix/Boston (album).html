n68''Boston'' was released by Epic Records on August 25, 1976.<ref>{{cite news |last=Zito |first=Tom |date=December 13, 1976 |title=Boston Group and How It Grew |newspaper=Los Angeles Times |page=F20 |quote=On Aug. 23, 1976, their first LP simply titled "Boston" was shipped to record stores. }}</ref> Few industry insiders thought a guitar-heavy rock record could make much of a dent in the charts as [[disco]] ruled the airwaves at the time.<ref name="GW">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.guitarworld.com/boston-feelin-satisfied|title=Boston: Feelin' Satisfied|date=October 2006|magazine=[[Guitar World]]|access-date=August 14, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927140239/http://www.guitarworld.com/boston-feelin-satisfied|archive-date=2013-09-27|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album broke out of [[Cleveland]] first, and the following week, it had been added at 392 stations.<ref name="RS"/> Had the record been unsuccessful, Scholz, then 29, planned to abandon his rock and roll dream; he still worked at Polaroid during the first few weeks of the record's success.<ref name="MG"/> Scholz felt pessimistic about the success until the album sold 200,000 copies. "And all of a sudden I realized I was in the music business," he told ''[[Rolling Stone]]''. "I got word on what the sales figures were while I was still at Polaroid full-time. It wasn't easy staying there two more weeks."<ref name="RS">{{cite magazine|title=The Band from the Platinum Basement|author=Cameron Crowe|date= August 10, 1978|magazine=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> Critics were kind to ''Boston''; 70The album was certified gold two months after its release, and sold another 500,000 copies within 30 days, going platinum for the first time in November 1976. By January 1977, the debut disc sold two million copies, making it one of the fastest selling debut albums in rock history.<ref name="GM"/> "More Than a Feeling" became a hit single on both [[AM radio|AM]] [[Top 40]] stations (with its second verse deleted for time constraints), and on [[FM radio|FM]] "[[Album-oriented rock|AOR]]" stations (with the second verse left intact).<ref name="GM"/> "I was at Polaroid when I first heard 'More Than A Feeling' on the radio," said Tom Scholz. "I was listening to somebody else's radio. The first week the album came out, it did better than I expected."<ref name="GM"/> Epic Records was pleased with their new acquisition—Boston and another new band, [[Wild Cherry (band)|Wild Cherry]], were among Epic's biggest success stories of 1976.<ref name="GM"/> The album was afforded several accolades, including a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for [[Best New Artist]]. Boston sold six million albums, [[8-track tape|8-tracks]] and cassettes by December 1977.<ref name="GM"/> For massive popularity, Boston was considered to rival established stars such as [[Peter Frampton]], [[Fleetwood Mac]] and [[Stevie Wonder]].<ref name="GP77">{{cite magazine|title=Overnight Success|date= August 1977|magazine=Guitar Player}}</ref>70The album was certified gold two months after its release, and sold another 500,000 copies within 30 days, going platinum for the first time in November 1976.
