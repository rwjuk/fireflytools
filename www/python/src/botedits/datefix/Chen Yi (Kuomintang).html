130==Later career==130==Later career==
t131Following his dismissal from the post of [[Taiwan Province|Taiwan]] Governor-General, Chen was employed as a consultant. In June 1948, he took the position of provincial chairman of [[Zhejiang]] province. In November, he released over a hundred communists scheduled to be executed. In January 1949, Chen Yi thought the KMT position was untenable, so to rescue the 18 million residents of the Nanjing-Shanghai-Hangzhou region from a meaningless war, he attempted to defect to the Chinese Communist Party. Along with his defection, he attempted to induce the garrison military commander [[Tang Enbo]] to surrender to the Communist Party. However, Tang informed [[Chiang Kai-shek]] that Chen had advised him to rebel against the Kuomintang.<ref name=ChenX>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Formosa Chief Executed As Traitor |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1917&dat=19500619&id=yaE0AAAAIBAJ&pg=820,2825535 |newspaper=Schenectady Gazette |agency=AP |date={{date|1950-06-18|mdy}} |access-date={{date|2014-11-13|mdy}} }}</ref> Chiang immediately relieved Chen's chairmanship on the charge of collaboration with the Communists. In April 1950, Chen Yi was escorted to Taiwan, and later imprisoned in [[Keelung]]. In May 1950, alleged for espionage case, Chiang Kai-shek ordered the Taiwan military court to sentence Chen Yi to death.
