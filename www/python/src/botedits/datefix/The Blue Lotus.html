80==Critical analysis==80==Critical analysis==
81''The Blue Lotus'' is widely regarded as one of the best Tintin adventures. Jean-Marc and Randy Lofficier commented that ''The Blue Lotus'' was "unarguably Hergé's first masterpiece".{{sfn|Lofficier|Lofficier|2002|p=35}} They felt that for the first time in the series, "one senses that the story has become important" as Tintin first expresses "a purpose, a mission" to his adventure.{{sfn|Lofficier|Lofficier|2002|p=36}} Commenting on the character Chang, they thought that he was an "endearing personality" despite having little relevance to the plot, also believing that Dawson and Gibbons were the most loathsome characters in the ''Adventure''.{{sfn|Lofficier|Lofficier|2002|p=34}} They compared the scene in which the Japanese invaded China with that in ''Tintin in America'' where the U.S. army force Native Americans off of their land, and praised the linear illustrations of the story, although also opined that the original black-and-white version was better than its colour counterpart.{{sfn|Lofficier|Lofficier|2002|p=36}} Overall, they awarded ''The Blue Lotus'' four stars out of five.{{sfn|Lofficier|Lofficier|2002|p=36}}81''The Blue Lotus'' is widely regarded as one of the best Tintin adventures.
