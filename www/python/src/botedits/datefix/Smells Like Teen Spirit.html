129==Parodies and covers==129==Parodies and covers==
130"Smells Like Teen Spirit" has inspired a few parodies. [["Weird Al" Yankovic]] parodied the song in 1992 with "[[Smells Like Nirvana]]", a song about Nirvana itself. Cobain quickly gave permission, but asked, "It's not about food, is it?" Yankovic answered, "No, it's about how nobody can understand your lyrics." Upon hearing the parody, Cobain and his band members laughed hysterically. Yankovic has said Cobain told him he realized that Nirvana had "made it" when he heard the parody.<ref>{{cite web| date = September 19, 2006 | title=Weird Al Yankovic Dishes On James Blunt, Discusses His Role As the Whitest, Nerdiest Rock Star Ever |work=Rolling Stone | url= https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/weird-al-yankovic-dishes-on-james-blunt-discusses-his-role-as-the-whitest-nerdiest-rock-star-ever-20060919 | access-date=April 5, 2011}}</ref> In 1995, the [[queercore]] band [[Pansy Division]] recorded a parody of the song called "Smells Like Queer Spirit" for its ''[[Pile Up]]'' album. Pansy Division guitarist [[Jon Ginoli]] insisted that his band's version of the song was not a parody but "an affectionate tribute".<ref name="Teen Spirit at ten" /> [[Tori Amos]] recorded the song and released it in 1992 on her "[[Crucify (song)|Crucify]]" EP single.
