86San Mateo County bears the [[Spanish language|Spanish]] name for [[Matthew the Evangelist|Saint Matthew]]. As a place name, San Mateo appears as early as 1776 in the diaries of Anza and Font.<ref>{{Cite book|title=California Place Names|last=Gudde|first=Erwin G.|publisher=University Of California Press|year=2004|isbn=0-520-24217-3|edition=Fourth|pages=341}}</ref> Several local geographic features were also designated San Mateo on early maps including variously: a settlement, an [[arroyo (creek)|arroyo]], a headland jutting into the Pacific ([[Montara, California|Point Montara]]), and a large land holding ([[Rancho San Mateo]]).
