Eugene W. Kettering joined [[Winton Motor Carriage Company|Winton Engine]] in 1930, which was acquired by General Motors and was eventually incorporated into the [[General Motors Electro-Motive Division|General Motors Electro-Motive Division (EMD)]]. 71Charles Kettering built a house, [[Charles F. Kettering House|"Ridgeleigh Terrace"]], in 1914. According to local sources, this house was the first in the United States to have electric air conditioning.<ref name="Boyd1957p91">{{Harvp|Boyd|1957|p=91}}</ref> Ridgeleigh Terrace was the home of his son, Eugene Kettering, until his death. Eugene's wife, [[Virginia Weiffenbach Kettering|Virginia Kettering]], lived in the house for many years, restoring and redecorating it.
