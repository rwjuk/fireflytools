n154Initial reviews of ''Graceland'' were positive. 156Retrospective reviews have continued to be positive. According to [[AllMusic]]'s William Ruhlmann, "''Graceland'' became the standard against which subsequent musical experiments by major artists were measured."<ref name="am">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/graceland-mw0000195707|title=Graceland – Paul Simon|author=William Ruhlmann|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 22, 2014}}</ref> Joe Tangari of [[Pitchfork Media|''Pitchfork'']] wrote that "its songs transcend the context as listening experiences. These songs are astute and exciting, spit-shined with the gloss of production that bears a lot of hallmarks of the era but somehow has refused to age. Taken as a whole, the album offers tremendous insight into how we live in our world and how that changes as we get older."<ref name="p4k"/> Patrick Humphries of [[BBC Music]] wrote that "it may well stand as the pinnacle of his remarkable half-century career ... Simon fashioned a record which was truly, blindingly original, and – listening to it a quarter of a century on – modern and timeless."<ref name="humphries">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/reviews/983q|title=Paul Simon Graceland: 25th Anniversary Edition Review|date=2012|access-date=November 21, 2014|publisher=[[BBC Music]]|author=Patrick Humphries}}</ref> Andy Gill of ''[[The Independent]]'' wrote: "The character of the base music here is overwhelming: complex, ebullient and life-affirming, and in yoking this intricate dance music to his sophisticated New Yorker sensibility, Simon created a transatlantic bridge that neither pandered to nor patronised either culture."<ref name="ind12"/>156Retrospective reviews have continued to be positive.
